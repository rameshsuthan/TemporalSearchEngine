Thomas Eric Duncan, the first Ebola patient diagnosed in the U.S., was treated at Texas Health Presbyterian Hospital in Dallas, which was not – and is not currently – recognized as a designated treatment facility.The Dallas hospital has come under fire for initially sending Duncan home with antibiotics in September before admitting him two days later. He was confirmed to have Ebola on Sept. 30 and died at the hospital a week later.Two of Duncan’s nurses later were diagnosed with Ebola: Nina Pham on Oct. 12 and Amber Vinson on Oct. 15.Vinson was flown to Emory University Hospital in Atlanta, the facility that this summer successfully treated the first two American Ebola patients: Dr. Kent Brantly and Nancy Writebol.Pham was flown to the National Institutes of Health facility in Maryland.
