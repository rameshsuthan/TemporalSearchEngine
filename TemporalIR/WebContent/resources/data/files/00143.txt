If you’re even a casual consumer of news, you’ve likely heard plenty about New York City resident Eric Silverman, who was hospitalized recently at Mt. Sinai hospital after displaying symptoms of Ebola. While the media went into overdrive, it turns out that the Brooklyn man – who recently returned from Sierra Leone – was likely suffering from malaria. The mistake is understandable, since the two diseases display similar symptoms - such as fever, headache, and aching joints. As The Gothamist headline joked, “Ebola Man" Probably Just Had Boring Malaria. Unlike Ebola, we have simple cost-effective treatments for malaria. They key is getting them in time, since kids can die within the first 24 hours of the onset of malaria symptoms.
