Cholera can be treated simply and successfully by immediately replacing the fluids and salts lost through vomiting and diarrhea—with prompt rehydration, less than one percent of cholera patients die.Cholera victims are always treated with oral rehydration solutions—prepackaged mixtures of sugars and salts that are mixed with water and drunk in large amounts. Severe cases will need these fluids to be replaced intravenously via a drip, and antibiotics are sometimes administered.
