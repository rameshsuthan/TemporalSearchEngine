The governors of New York and New Jersey announced mandatory quarantines for medical workers returning from West Africa. The governor of Illinois also ordered a 21-day quarantine for high-risk individuals. Federal, state and local officials are identifying hospitals to be used as Ebola treatment centers. A full list is expected later this week. Of the nation’s 100 state public health laboratories tests, 24 are ready to test for Ebola. The five United States airports are screening travelers from West Africa for fever are: Kennedy International, Washington Dulles International, O’Hare International, Hartsfield-Jackson International and Newark Liberty International.
