In Sierra Leone, a Fine Line Between Malaria and Ebola Symptoms
For many, the two diseases start out the same: a low fever, an aching body, vomiting and headaches. Sierra Leone's Ministry of Health noticed that many of the country's suspected Ebola cases were in fact malaria. So it launched a mass prophylaxis campaign, targeting many people who had never taken anti-malarials before. From Freetown, Cinnatus Dumbaya reports.
