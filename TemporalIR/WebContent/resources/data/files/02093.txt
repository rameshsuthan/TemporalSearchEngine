The case fatality proportion among patients in West Africa with a known outcome is about 71% (ranges from 46% in Nigeria to 69-72% in Guinea, Sierra Leone and Liberia). Risk factors significantly associated with a fatal outcome in the affected countries in West Africa include, age >45 years old, unexplained bleeding, and a number of other signs and symptoms (diarrhea, chest pain, cough, difficulty breathing, difficulty swallowing, conjunctivitis, sore throat, confusion, hiccups, and coma or unconsciousness).
